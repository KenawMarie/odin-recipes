Ethiopian Kitfo

Description

A culinary specialty of the Gurage region of Central Ethiopia, kitfo is a rich and flavorful dish made with minced raw lean beef, spices (the classic Ethiopian spice blend known as mitmita), and an herb-infused clarified butter known as niter kibbeh. Kitfo is renowned for its bold flavor and luxurious texture.

kitfo incorporates warm, seasoned clarified butter, which makes for a wholly unique flavor experience. The niter kibbeh should be warmed through enough to infuse the meat with its flavors, but should not be so hot that it begins to cook the beef.

Ingredients

  • 3 lbs (1.36kg) beef, freshly ground (lean, without fat)
  • ¼ cup (60ml/56g) niter kibbeh (Ethiopian clarified butter)
  • 1 tsp (2g) korerima (Ethiopian cardamom)
  • 1 tsp (2g) mitmita (Ethiopian chili powder)
  • 1 tsp (5g) salt
  • ½ tsp koseret

Steps

  1. Begin by chopping 1 lb of beef into small pieces.
  2. Add the beef to a bowl.
  3. Add 2 tablespoons of mitmita to the bowl.
  4. Mix the mitmita into the beef, ensuring that it is evenly distributed.
  5. Add 2 teaspoons of korerima (cardamom) to the bowl.
  6. Mix the kororema into the beef, ensuring that it is evenly distributed.
  7. Add 1/2 teaspoon of koseret to the bowl.
  8. Mix the koseret into the beef, ensuring that it is evenly distributed.
  9. Add 1/2 teaspoon of salt to the bowl.
  10. Mix the salt into the beef, ensuring that it is evenly distributed.
  11. In a separate pan, melt 4 tablespoons of kibe (clarified butter).
  12. Pour the melted kibe over the beef.
  13. Rub the kibe into the beef, using food-safe gloves.
  14. Once the meat is evenly coated in the kibe, serve with ayib (a mild cheese) and/or gomen (cooked collard greens).
