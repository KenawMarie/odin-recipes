Ethiopian Cabbage Dish

Description

Ethiopian cabbage dishes, often called Atakilt Wat or Tikil Gomen, are flavorful vegetable stews featuring cabbage, potatoes, and carrots, simmered in a spiced sauce.

Ingredients

  • ½ cup olive oil or niter kibbeh(clarified butter)
  • 4 medium carrots, thinly sliced
  • 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
  • ½ head green cabbage, shredded
  • 1 teaspoon sea salt
  • ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • ½ teaspoon ground cumin, or to taste
  • ¼ teaspoon ground turmeric, or to taste
  • ginger and garlic
  • 5 medium potatoes, peeled and cut into 1-inch cubes

Steps

  1. Heat olive oil or niter kibbeh, a clarified butter in a pan over medium heat. Add chopped onions and sauté until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Stir in diced carrots and potatoes. Cook for a few minutes, allowing them to soften slightly.
  3. Add turmeric, cumin, salt, and pepper to the vegetables. You can also add ginger and garlic at this stage for extra flavor.
  4. Add the shredded cabbage to the pan and mix well with the other vegetables and spices.
  5. Cover the pan and cook over medium-low heat, stirring occasionally, until the cabbage is tender but not mushy. Add a splash of water or broth if the vegetables start to stick.
  6. add berbere (a spice blend) or jalapeño peppers for heat. You can also add a little more oil or butter for richness.
  7. Serve Cabbage Dish (the Tikil Gomen hot), often alongside injera (a spongy flatbread) and other Ethiopian dishes.
